Keyboards

Layout and typing speed

  • QWERTY is the standard in the English-speaking world
  • Dvorak does seem more efficient, but not worth the effort needed for adoption
    • Why the Dvorak keyboard didn't take over the world - Engineer Guy
  • For truly high speed typing, workers like court typists use stenography, which can type over 2-3x the speed of QWERTY
    • Plover: Thought to Text at 230 WPM
      • Plover - Open Steno Project
    • Programming with steno
      • Is typing really the bottleneck for programming?
      • "SQL, Lisp, and Haskell are the only programming languages that I've seen where one spends more time thinking than typing." - Phillip Greenspun
  • Over 90% of Japanese typists use QWERTY-based Romaji input
    • Kana input theoretically uses half the keystrokes (generally 1 kana = 2 romaji)
    • You often need to switch back to Romaji for typing English letters, so switching typing modes physically and mentally negates the speed benefits for small amounts of text
    • Is modern kana input ergonomic? Keys are spread across four rows
    • Thumb shift is an improvement on kana based keyboards
