Mahavir Feldspar

Mahavir is a potassium (potash) Feldspar imported from India.

  • Mahavir is used as a replacement potassium feldspar since Custer Feldspar is no longer being mined as of 2024
    • Custer has slightly more silica, so a 1:1 substitution may cause changes in glazes.
    • An approximate substitution for Custer:
      • 90.5% Mahavir Feldspar
      • 6.5% Silica
      • 3% EP Kaolin
  • Mahavir is naturally thixiotropic.
    • Pure Mahavir can be suspended in water with a ratio of 100g Mahavir to 70g water. It still settles quite quickly though. This can be fixed with some Bentonite or Epsom Salts
    • Glazes low in clay should see improved dipping applications if Custer Feldspar is substituted with Mahavir.
