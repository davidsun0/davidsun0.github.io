Kagawa

Kagawa Prefecture (formerly Sanuki Province) is Japan's smallest prefecture by land area and occupies the north eastern part of Shikoku.

Kagawa's geography and climate is suited for growing wheat rather than rice. This wheat is used to make Sanuki Udon, which is famous across Japan.

  • In 2020, Kagawa passed a law (the first of its kind in Japan) limiting the video game and internet use by minors.
  • The rock Sanukite is named after Sanuki Province
  • Buddhist monk Kukai was born in Kagawa and is (falsely) believed to have invented Hiragana, one of the three major Japanese writing systems.
    • (Hiragana evolved from kanji, so it is incorrect to say someone invented it)
